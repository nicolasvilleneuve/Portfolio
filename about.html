About Me

I am a MSc student at uOttawa, a research assistant, and I hold a research affiliate position at Agriculture and Agri-Food Canada (AAFC/AAC). As a result, I am a master of time-management, and project execution. I have an unquenchable thirst for knowledge and a desire to accomplish at the highest level. I not only have a passion for programming, but a passion for technology itself. That said, when the blue light gives me a headache, I love to hike, do my work in the lab, and play with my dog, Laszlo.

The project underway towards my M.Sc degree is a multi-disciplinary and multi-departmental effort which allows me to leverage my capacity to lead, analyze complex problems, meticulously research, critically assess information sources, and strategic avenues to develop effective plans of action. This is accomplished through the creative use of data science upon analytical chemistry to identify the trends hidden within massive datasets. The revelation of these hidden trends informs important decisions in coordinating groups and must therefore be communicated effectively. the processing and interpretation of the massive datasets generated through my MSc project, was only made possible through the design and application of Machine Learning (ML) models.

Prior to applying devised models on my datasets, I experimented with those models on other available datasets (such as from ISLR, Kaggle, and the UCI ML repository). As such, I have accrued a wealth of experience in the application of ML models to datasets ranging in the vastness of their complexity and application (with my own dataset remaining one of the most complicated I have come across). In turn, my unique experience and robust education has allowed me to cultivate a skillset that would be well-suited to a Data Science position. By result of the programming languages I have learned over the past 4 years, however, I would be well suited to a Software Engineer, or Front-End/Web programmer, as well.
